# ID 106 research packet — complaint survey reading order

This held article is a record-reading guide for a family comparing facilities. It must not diagnose a facility, state that a complaint is proven, or tell a reader how an individual complaint will be resolved.

## Source and interpretation plan

| Official source | Use in the article | Specific contribution |
| --- | --- | --- |
| [Medicare Care Compare: health inspections](https://www.medicare.gov/care-compare/resources/nursing-home/health-inspections/) | Explain the difference between standard inspections, complaint inspections, citations, and the public inspection context. | A four-step reading order: event/date, cited concern, scope/severity context, then the current-answer question. |
| [CMS nursing-home enforcement](https://www.cms.gov/medicare/health-safety-standards/enforcement/nursing-home-enforcement) | Establish that survey/enforcement actions have defined processes and time context. | A boundary: a public entry is evidence to ask about, not evidence of a resident's current experience. |
| [CMS Provider Data Catalog](https://data.cms.gov/provider-data/topics/nursing-homes) | Verify fields/release context where public data are used. | A source-date note that keeps data release timing separate from the inspection event. |

## Unique editorial contract

- Reader job: decide what to read first when a facility has both standard and complaint-survey entries.
- Decision moment: a family sees an alarming record before a tour or discharge call.
- Answer claim: chronology and the nature of the finding must be understood before a family treats a record as a comparison verdict.
- Structure: four-step chronology card, neutral evidence note, tour-question conversion, and a correction path for missing dates.
- Non-overlap: does not teach filing a complaint, define repeated deficiencies, or compare ratings.
- Hold: retain `needs_human_review`; do not publish, index, serve ads, or fabricate reviewer confirmation.
