# ID 109 research packet — Medicare SNF days question

## Article boundary

This held draft will help a family prepare a pre-admission coverage call after a hospital stay. It will not rank facilities, determine Medicare eligibility, estimate a personal bill, or advise a family to accept or refuse a placement.

## Source ledger

| Source | Used for | Article-specific use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| [Medicare: Skilled nursing facility care](https://www.medicare.gov/coverage/skilled-nursing-facility-care) | Explain the qualifying inpatient-stay rule, the benefit-period limit, and the split between days 1–20, 21–100, and later days. | The call worksheet separates hospital status, benefit-period timing, and facility certification instead of treating “100 days” as a promise. |
| [Medicare: Nursing home care](https://www.medicare.gov/coverage/nursing-home-care) | Distinguish short-term skilled coverage from long-term custodial care. | The article's stop rule is triggered when the proposed stay is being described as long-term custodial care without a separate payment plan. |
| [Medicare: Find nursing-home care](https://www.medicare.gov/providers-services/original-medicare/nursing-homes/choosing) | Frame facility comparison as one input alongside care needs. | The final worksheet asks the care team to confirm the resident-specific plan rather than infer coverage from a facility profile. |

## Required body structure

1. “Four answers to obtain before a bed decision”: inpatient status, benefit-period status, certified-SNF status, and care-plan/payment handoff.
2. A dated call worksheet that distinguishes facts stated by the hospital, the facility, and the plan.
3. A scenario in which an observation stay is mistakenly counted as a qualifying inpatient stay; it must instruct the reader to verify, not make a coverage finding.
4. A boundary section stating that facility staff, a plan, and the treating team must confirm the person's situation.
5. A correction path for conflicting answers: record who answered, request the written/portal source, and pause any financial assumption.
