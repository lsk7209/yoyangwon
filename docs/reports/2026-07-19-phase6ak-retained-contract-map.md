# Caregos phase6ak retained editorial contract map

## Scope and stop condition

This is the contract map for the 18 retained representatives not yet rewritten. It is deliberately **not** a publication plan. Each record remains `needs_human_review` / held until its individual body, source ledger, quality validation, and independent human review are complete. The 92 suffix-only variants remain held for merge or a separately approved contract.

The source paths below are a research starting point, not a substitute for checking the live source on the day of a rewrite. The articles must not present Care Compare, CMS, Medicare, or facility data as bedside facts or individualized medical, legal, benefits, or financial advice.

| ID | Reader job and decision moment | Answer claim | Evidence plan | Non-overlap and structure |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 109 | A family is planning a post-hospital SNF stay and needs to ask the discharge team the coverage questions before accepting a bed. | A 100-day headline is not enough: eligibility, benefit-period timing, daily cost exposure, and the individual care plan must be checked separately. | Medicare SNF coverage page and current cost details; cite only dated official facts. | A pre-admission coverage-call worksheet; it does not rank facilities or explain Medicaid eligibility. |
| 106 | A reader sees both standard-survey and complaint entries and needs to know what order to read them in. | Read the event, cited concern, scope/severity context, and the facility's explanation before treating a complaint entry as a current verdict. | Medicare health-inspection explainer; CMS survey/enforcement material. | A record-reading sequence with a note-taking card; it does not tell someone how to file a complaint. |
| 108 | A family hears that a plan of correction exists and wants to know what follow-up question is meaningful. | A plan describes alleged corrective action; it is not proof that the condition is fixed, so follow-up must distinguish plan date, later survey, and current observation. | CMS enforcement FAQ / survey-enforcement chapter; later public inspection record where available. | A timeline reconstruction; it does not duplicate the deficiency-pattern glossary. |
| 116 | A family has a data point but cannot turn it into a respectful, answerable tour question. | A useful tour question names the public signal, asks for the current process, and records a verifiable response without assuming the data explains every resident experience. | CMS Provider Data Catalog/data dictionary and Medicare Care Compare guidance. | A signal-to-question converter with examples; it does not compare two facilities. |
| 118 | A reader sees repeated F-tags or citations and needs to decide whether there is a pattern worth asking about. | A pattern requires matching subject, time sequence, and scope/severity context; a repeated label alone is not a conclusion about current care. | Medicare health-inspection explanation; CMS survey/enforcement documentation. | A classification glossary with a three-part pattern test; it does not assess a specific facility. |
| 113 | A family must decide whether a closer facility is worth a tradeoff against care needs and visitation reliability. | Distance should be scored against the resident's visit-dependent needs, travel reliability, and backup plan—not as a universal mileage rule. | Medicare choosing-a-nursing-home guidance; facility contact/location data. | A weighted travel-and-care tradeoff sheet; it does not rate quality metrics. |
| 107 | A reader notices a fine date and survey date that differ and wants to avoid drawing the wrong timeline. | Enforcement, survey, processing, and posting dates answer different questions and should be recorded separately before comparison. | CMS enforcement page; Provider Data Catalog penalties dataset/data dictionary. | A date-key timeline diagram; it does not explain the full plan-of-correction process. |
| 122 | A family wants a medication-management question for a tour without asking staff to disclose private resident information. | Ask about the facility's medication-review process, change communication, and escalation path rather than demanding patient-specific details. | Medicare health-inspection guidance (medication management context); CMS resident-rights material. | A consent-aware conversation script; it does not give medication or treatment advice. |
| 119 | An adult child cannot visit overnight and needs to test the communication path before admission. | A call plan is credible only when it names who answers, when escalation occurs, how updates are documented, and what happens when the usual contact is unavailable. | CMS nursing-home resident-rights and staffing information; facility's stated contact policy. | A failure-mode call-tree exercise; it does not evaluate staffing-hour data. |
| 104 | A family sees turnover data and needs to ask a specific question rather than infer quality from one number. | Turnover is a prompt to ask about continuity, supervision, orientation, and weekend coverage; it is not a standalone quality judgment. | CMS Provider Information data dictionary and current Provider Data Catalog field definitions. | A question script paired with a response-record sheet; it does not compare staffing ratings. |
| 111 | A private-pay family needs to surface future-rate risk before signing an agreement. | Obtain the written rate, included services, change-notice rule, and payment alternatives; do not assume one facility's terms apply elsewhere. | CMS/Medicare payment and consumer guidance; state-specific resources only after state verification. | A contract-document checklist; it does not provide legal or financial advice. |
| 114 | A reader is matching a Care Compare record to a facility and needs to avoid name/address mix-ups. | Use the CMS Certification Number with current address and provider identity fields; a name match alone is not sufficiently reliable. | CMS Provider Information dataset/data dictionary. | A record-matching protocol with mismatch stop rules; it does not interpret ratings. |
| 117 | A family sees resident-day terminology and wants to translate it into a concrete question about the daily routine. | Aggregate resident-day data describes a reporting measure, not an individual's day; use it to form an observation question and verify it on a visit. | CMS Provider Information data dictionary; relevant measure methodology. | A plain-language translation plus observation log; it does not analyze occupancy or financing. |
| 123 | A family is tempted to choose the highest-ranked facility without checking fit for the resident. | Ratings can narrow a list, but the final decision also needs resident-specific care needs, visit access, communication fit, and current confirmation. | Medicare Care Compare/five-star guidance and choosing-a-nursing-home guidance. | A resident-fit decision ledger; it does not explain star-rating refresh mechanics. |
| 105 | A reader sees scope/severity language and needs to make a factual family note without exaggerating it. | Record what the citation says, when it was found, scope/severity context, and what must be verified now; do not convert the label into a claim of present harm. | Medicare health-inspections guide; CMS survey/enforcement documentation. | A neutral evidence-note template; it does not identify a deficiency pattern across years. |
| 115 | A family is comparing sources updated at different times and needs a safe decision rule. | Treat source date, observation period, processing date, and facility response date as separate fields; stop the comparison if dates cannot be aligned. | CMS data collection intervals; Provider Data Catalog release metadata and data dictionary. | A source-date reconciliation table; it does not interpret fines or citations. |
| 101 | A reader wants to know when a star rating is current enough to use for shortlisting. | Verify the rating's source/update context and pair it with current visit and contact evidence; a rating is a screening tool, not an admission decision. | CMS five-star methodology / Medicare Care Compare explanation; Provider Data Catalog release metadata. | A refresh-check decision tree; it does not repeat resident-fit selection. |
| 121 | A family needs to ask about therapy discharge planning before a rehab stay begins. | Ask how goals, progress updates, discharge criteria, caregiver training, and follow-up are coordinated for this resident; facility claims require confirmation with the care team. | Medicare SNF coverage page and CMS resident-rights guidance. | A care-transition question sequence; it does not estimate coverage days or treatment outcomes. |

## Required per-record evidence before any status change

1. Replace the inherited matrix body with an individually written body that follows this row's structure.
2. Add an article-specific editorial contract and fact-traceable source ledger.
3. Run the article-quality validator against the approved corpus and inspect cross-draft overlap.
4. Leave `needs_human_review` in place until an actual named reviewer completes the review; never use a synthetic reviewer confirmation.
